About

I'm a theoretical physicist, software developer, and musician living in San Francisco, CA.

I'm interested in mathematics, quantum gravity, statistical mechanics and inference, and artificial intelligence.

I'm excited about using ideas in statistical physics to inform and help explain more "traditional" methods in machine learning. I want to better understand how structure emerges in these seemingly complex systems, and discover principled techniques for designing the next generation of algorithms and architectures.

I have a Ph.D. in theoretical physics from the University of Texas at Austin, where I studied with Willy Fischler and Elena Carceres.

I got a B.S in physics from the University of California San Diego.
