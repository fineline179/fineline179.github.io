About

I'm a theoretical physicist, software developer, data scientist, and musician living in San Francisco, CA.

My academic interests are fairly broad, and include topics in mathematics, holographic quantum gravity, statistical mechanics, and machine learning.

I'm excited about using ideas from statistical physics to inform and help explain more "traditional" methods in machine learning. I want to better understand how structure emerges in these complex systems, and discover principled techniques to design a new generation of algorithms and architectures.

I did my Ph.D. in theoretical physics in the theory group at UT Austin, where I studied with Willy Fischler and Elena Caceres. My research focused on building string-theoretic models of quantum chromodynamics and cosmological inflationary mechanisms.

My B.S in physics is from UC San Diego.
