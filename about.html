About

I'm a theoretical physicist, software developer, and musician living in San Francisco, CA.

I'm broadly interested (and accordingly sparsely published) in mathematics, quantum gravity, statistical mechanics and inference, and machine learning.

I'm excited about using ideas in statistical physics to inform and help explain more "traditional" methods in machine learning. I want to better understand how structure emerges in these seemingly complex systems, and discover principled techniques to design a new generation of algorithms and architectures.

I received my Ph.D. in theoretical physics from UT Austin, where I studied with Willy Fischler and Elena Carceres.

My B.S in physics is from UC San Diego.
